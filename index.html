Frederick Law Olmsted:

the father of landscape architecture

Personal and Early Life

Frederick Law Olmsted was born on April 26, 1822 in Hartford, Connecticut. From an early age, Frederick Law was interested in the natural world. He worked for a time as a merchant and then journalist before moving to his family farm on Staten Island, New York. Olmsted's career as a journalist allowed him to travel in Europe where he was impressed by the parks in England.

In 1858 Olmsted entered and won a contest to design a park in New York with his friend Calver Vaux. This was Olmsted's first attempt at landscape design, and that park is Central Park in New York City.

In 1895 Olmsted retired from his career as a landscape architect and lived until 1903. Olmsted's sons continued their father's work as the nation's first landscape architecture firm.

Notable Parks

Among the most well-known of Frederick Law Olmsted's great parks is New York City's Central Park which he designed with Calver Vaux.

    Other notable designs by Olmsted include:
  • Stanford University's Campus
  • Washington D.C. National Zoo
  • Niagra Falls State Park
  • Prospect Park in NYC
  • 1893 Chicago Columbian Exposition Grounds

Influence

Frederick Law Olmsted was one of the earlies major proponents to the conservation movement in the United States.
